Spiderverse

Moves through the spiderverse

"Spider-Verse" is a captivating and intricate storyline in the world of Marvel Comics that delves into the fascinating concept of a multiverse filled with diverse versions of Spider-Man. This narrative arc introduces readers to a plethora of Spider-People from various dimensions, each with their own unique backgrounds and abilities. The central conflict arises with the emergence of the formidable Inheritors, a cosmic threat that hunts Spider-Totems for their life force. As the different Spider-Men and Women are forced to unite and form the Spider-Army, readers are treated to a thrilling and visually stunning adventure, rich in action and character development. "Spider-Verse" not only showcases the heroism and camaraderie of these web-slinging characters but also explores the idea that anyone can be a hero, regardless of their background or dimension, making it a beloved and thought-provoking addition to the Spider-Man mythology.

Spider-man - Friendly Neighborhood

Spider-Man: Spider-Man is the superhero identity of Peter Parker. He gained his superhuman abilities after being bitten by a radioactive spider. His powers include superhuman strength, agility, and the ability to cling to walls. He also has a "spider-sense" that alerts him to danger. Spider-Man is known for his acrobatic fighting style, web-swinging through New York City, and using web-shooters to create various web-based tools and weapons. He is often depicted wearing a red and blue costume with a spider emblem on his chest.

Spider-Man is one of Marvel Comics' most iconic and popular characters, known for his strong moral code and his struggles to balance his personal life with his responsibilities as a superhero.

Venom - The V-Man

Venom is a fictional character from Marvel Comics who is an alien symbiote that can bond with a human host and give them superpowers. Venom was originally introduced as a living alien costume that Spider-Man wore, but later became one of his enemies when he bonded with Eddie Brock, a journalist who hated Spider-Man. Venom has also been a hero and an antihero at different times, and has had many other hosts besides Brock, such as Mac Gargan, Flash Thompson, and Dylan Brock. Venom has also spawned several offspring, such as Carnage, who is his most dangerous enemy. Venom has appeared in comic books, animated shows, video games, and movies, such as the 2018 film Venom and its sequel Venom: Let There Be Carnage.

Miles Morales

Miles is a character that appears in different stories and media, but the most famous one is Miles Morales, who is a version of Spider-Man from Marvel Comics. Miles Morales is a teenager from Brooklyn who gets bitten by a genetically modified spider and gains superpowers similar to Peter Parker, the original Spider-Man. Miles has to balance his life as a student, a son, a friend, and a hero, while also dealing with enemies like the Green Goblin, Venom, and Carnage. Miles is inspired by Peter’s legacy and tries to live up to his motto: “With great power comes great responsibility.” Miles is also a multicultural character, as he is of African-American and Puerto Rican descent, and he speaks both English and Spanish. Miles has appeared in comic books, animated shows, video games, and movies, such as Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse and Spider-Man: No Way Home
